COVID 19 and Mortality (R & Forecasting time-series data )

Did states that had more people travel during the pandemic also coincide with more deaths?



Not really, but this data includes COVID-19 mortality from early 2020 through 2023, so it's not specific enough to show effects of early pandemic behavior (increased travel) on mortality outcomes. Let’s look at mortality rates in 2020 only.


Now a more interesting story emerges. This graph is positively correlated with COVID-19 mortality rates! The R2 value is about 0.20 with p < 0.05. Although the effect size is moderate, this suggests that travel behavior in early pandemic years was somewhat correlated with worse COVID-19 outcomes.


Forecasting COVID-19 cases using autoregressive models


Let’s look at time-series forecasting using ARIMA on a heavily hit province, Ontario, Canada.


Forecast Date Predicted Cases
2023-01-01 1132
2023-01-02 1104
2023-01-03 1092
2023-01-04 1075
2023-01-05 1078

ARIMA modeling with seasonal terms allowed for forecasting new case numbers across several weeks. Note that the pandemic is in a more stable phase by 2023, making this relatively easy to forecast!
